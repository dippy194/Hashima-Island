Hashima Island

The Forgotten Ghost Island of Japan

History of Hashima

Hashima Island, also known as Gunkanjima or "Battleship Island," was once a thriving coal mining facility off the coast of Nagasaki, Japan. It symbolized rapid industrialization, but was abandoned in 1974 and left to decay. Its eerie, fortress-like silhouette tells a haunting story of labor, hardship, and history frozen in time.
