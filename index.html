Hashima Island

Introduction

Hashima Island, also known as Gunkanjima or "Battleship Island," is located off the coast of Nagasaki, Japan. It was once a thriving coal mining facility operated by Mitsubishi from 1887 to 1974.

Industrial Expansion

The island became a symbol of rapid industrialization during the Meiji Restoration. Concrete buildings were constructed to house the growing population of workers.

Forced Labor History

During World War II, Hashima Island was used to house Korean and Chinese forced laborers under harsh conditions.

Abandonment

With the decline of coal as an energy source, the island was abandoned in 1974, leaving behind a ghost town of decaying concrete.

Modern Recognition

In 2015, Hashima was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site, sparking debates over how its wartime history is acknowledged.
