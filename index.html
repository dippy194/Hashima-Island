Historical Background

Operated by Mitsubishi, Hashima was a coal mining hub from 1887 until 1974. The island reached peak population density in the 1950s with over 5,000 residents on a space the size of 15 acres. Voices of Survivors

According to Yonhap News, many Korean survivors remember harsh labor conditions under Japanese occupation during World War II. Survivors recount being forced to work in life-threatening conditions with little food or rest.

Timeline of Key Events

1887

Coal discovered and mining begins on Hashima Island.

1940s

Korean and Chinese laborers forcibly brought to the island during WWII.

1974

Coal reserves depleted; Mitsubishi officially closes the mine.

2009

UNESCO begins consideration for World Heritage status.

2015

Hashima designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site with criticism over lack of wartime context.
