Hashima Island

The Forgotten Ghost Island of Japan

What is Hashima Island?

Also known as Gunkanjima or "Battleship Island," Hashima was once a thriving coal mining facility owned by Mitsubishi. It was famous for having one of the highest population densities in the world during its peak.

Historical Timeline

  • 1887: Coal discovered on the island.
  • 1916: Japan’s first large concrete building constructed here.
  • 1940s: Forced labor during WWII.
  • 1974: Mine closed, island abandoned.

Modern Legacy

Today, Hashima Island stands as a haunting symbol of industrial history. It was featured in the James Bond movie Skyfall and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
