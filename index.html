Hashima Island

Industry, Abandonment, and the Shadow of History

Introduction

Hashima Island, or Gunkanjima ("Battleship Island"), is a small abandoned island off the coast of Nagasaki, Japan. It became a symbol of rapid industrialization and dark wartime history. Owned by Mitsubishi, it operated as a coal mining facility from 1890 to 1974. Though it helped power Japan's economy, the island was also a site of forced labor during WWII, leaving behind a complex legacy.

Forced Labor During WWII

Between 1939 and 1945, over 800 Korean and Chinese laborers were forcibly taken to Hashima. Conditions were brutal: long underground shifts, unsafe tunnels, and poor rations. Survivors have recounted violent treatment and constant fear. Many died from overwork or accidents.
