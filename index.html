Hashima Island

From Industrial Power to Abandoned Prison

The Japanese Perspective

Hashima Island, also known as Gunkanjima or "Battleship Island," is a haunting relic of Japan's industrial boom. Once home to thousands of workers mining undersea coal, it now stands deserted off the coast of Nagasaki. Japan presents it as a symbol of industrial prowess and modernization during the early 20th century (Japan National Tourism Organization).

The Hidden Reality

What Japan’s official narratives often downplay is that many of these workers were Koreans forcibly brought during Japan’s colonial rule. Labor conditions were dangerous, exploitative, and inhumane. A 2017 report by Yonhap News revealed efforts by Korean survivors and historians to push back against Japan’s version of events, calling for recognition and memorials (Yonhap News).
